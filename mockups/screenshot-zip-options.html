Zip-Framed Screenshots

Each option uses vertical lines as the framing device, consistent with the Barnett Newman zip motif. The horizontal border-top in the gallery is removed in all options — the zip line(s) alone define the transition. Each shows a light-UI screenshot (Magpie) and a dark-UI screenshot (Ibis) to test both extremes.

Option A

Flanking Zips

Two vertical accent-colored zips frame the screenshot on both sides, extending the full height of the gallery. The image floats between them like a color field between two zips in "Who's Afraid of Red, Yellow and Blue." No horizontal lines anywhere.

Option D

Field Bands

Two thin accent zips overlay the screenshot at fixed positions near the edges — like Newman's multi-zip paintings ("The Name II", "Anna's Light"). The lines bleed over the image content itself, asserting the portfolio's visual language over the app's own UI. More aggressive, but unmistakably ties every screenshot to the design system.

Option E

Zip Margin

The screenshot is pushed right, leaving a generous empty dark field on the left. A single zip divides the empty field from the image — two color fields separated by a zip, the most literal Newman interpretation. The negative space breathes and gives the screenshot room. The left field could later hold thumbnail navigation vertically if needed.

Comparison

A — Flanking Zips

Symmetric. Two zips create a bounded field. Clear containment. Slightly formal — feels like the screenshot is exhibited between two pillars.

B — Single Left Zip

Asymmetric, minimal. One zip is enough to mark ownership. The open right edge lets the image dissolve into the background. Lightest touch.

C — Center Zip Continuity

Structural. The central zip runs through everything — hero, project rows, gallery, detail text, footer. Nothing interrupts it. Most architecturally consistent, and gives the gallery the same split-layout DNA as the rest of the page.

D — Field Bands

Assertive. The zips lay over the image, not beside it. The portfolio's language dominates the screenshot's own UI. Bold but potentially distracting if the bands cross important content.

E — Zip Margin

Most literal Newman. Two fields (empty dark + screenshot) divided by a zip. The negative space is the design. The left field could hold vertical thumbnail navigation later.
