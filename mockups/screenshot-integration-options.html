Option A

Dark Browser Chrome

Wraps each screenshot in a styled dark browser/app window frame. The chrome acts as a visual buffer between the bright content and the dark background, making the screenshots feel like apps running inside the portfolio. The accent color appears on the first dot and as a bottom border.

Option C

Recessed Panel with Dimming

Screenshots sit in a recessed "well" with an inset shadow, slightly dimmed (brightness reduced to 88%). This pulls bright screenshots closer to the dark palette without distorting them. The accent color appears as a thin gradient line across the top. Hover restores full brightness — inviting interaction. The most subtle option.

Summary

A — Browser Chrome

Best for: framing the screenshots as real software. The dark titlebar creates a natural transition zone between bright content and dark background. Works especially well for Strix (already has macOS chrome) and web apps.

B — Perspective Glow

Best for: visual drama. The accent glow ties each screenshot to its project color, and the vignette helps blend edges. But might feel heavy in a gallery with 4 thumbnails auto-cycling.

C — Recessed + Dim

Best for: minimal treatment. The dimming solves the brightness mismatch without adding visual weight. The hover-to-brighten creates a subtle interactive moment. Lightest touch on the existing design.
